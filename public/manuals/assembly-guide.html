OFFICIAL ASSEMBLY & RC INTEGRATION GUIDE

SECTION 1: UNBOXING & INVENTORY

Congratulations on purchasing your premium high-performance racing kit! Every kit ships with the full brick assembly set plus the integrated RC (Remote Control) motorization upgrade.

STEP 1.1
Verify All Parts Bags
Carefully open the box and check all numbered parts bags (1–12) are present. Do not open all bags at once — open each numbered bag only when the step instructions require it.
STEP 1.2
Check the RC Power Kit Box
Locate the separate sealed RC components box inside the main package. It should contain:
  • 1× Rechargeable Lithium Smart Battery Box
  • 1× 2.4GHz Wireless Remote Controller
  • 2× High-Torque L-Motors (for driving)
  • 1× Servo Motor (for steering)
  • 1× USB Charging Cable

PRO TIP Prepare a clean, wide workspace with containers or small bowls to separate pins, gears, and small parts by bag number. This saves time during the build.

SECTION 2: CHASSIS & RC MOTOR INTEGRATION

RC MOTORS INCLUDED IN EVERY KIT
⚠ IMPORTANT The RC motors must be built directly INTO the chassis during Steps 12–45 of the official brick instructions. Do not finish the body shell before inserting the motors. Refer to the images below for correct motor placement.
STEP 2.1
Build the Central Differential Axle
Begin the build with the main central gearbox axle. This is the backbone of the car that connects the front steering to the rear drive system. Make sure all gear teeth are properly meshed before moving forward.
Black Warrior V12 — Chassis and drivetrain visible before body panels
STEP 2.2
Mount the Steering Servo Motor
Place the Servo Motor in the front compartment. Link the gear drive to the steering rack. The servo cable should route along the right-side channel toward the battery box location.
STEP 2.3
Install the Dual L-Motors
Place the two High-Torque L-Motors in the rear engine bay, connecting them to the rear differential gear system. Ensure the motor shafts click into the cross-axle connectors firmly.
Green Bull — Rear engine bay and motor placement area
Pink Bull — Motor cables routed along chassis channels
STEP 2.4
Route Power Cables & Install Battery Box
Route all motor power cables along the guide channels toward the center of the chassis. Install the Smart Battery Box in the cockpit area where it will be protected yet accessible for USB charging. The battery box lid should face upward for easy access.

SECTION 3: PAIRING & TESTING

Before mounting the body panels, test the electronics to ensure everything is connected correctly.

STEP 3.1
Power On & Pair
1. Turn on the Smart Battery Box (green indicator light turns on).
2. Switch on the 2.4GHz remote controller.
3. Wait 3 seconds — the LED on the battery box should flash twice, confirming pairing.
STEP 3.2
Test Drive Functions
1. Test steering: push the left joystick left/right.
2. Test drive: push the right joystick forward/backward.
3. If movement is reversed, flip the toggle switch on the battery box.
PRO TIP Place the chassis on a raised surface (like a book) so the wheels can spin freely during testing. This prevents the car from driving off the table!

SECTION 4: BODY PANELS & FINISHING

STEP 4.1
Side Panels & Doors
Build and snap-fit the side panels and doors onto the chassis. Ensure the door hinges align properly. On Lamborghini models, the scissor doors should open vertically without obstruction.
Cyber Porsche 911 — Body panels fully assembled
Blue Bolide — Hood and splitter fitted
STEP 4.2
Front Splitter & Hood
Attach the front splitter and hood cover. For models with opening hoods, ensure the hinge mechanism clicks into place before pressing down.
STEP 4.3
Rear Wing Assembly
Complete the build with the rear wing assembly. Active rear wings (on models like the 911 Turbo S) should be tested for smooth deployment before final attachment.
STEP 4.4
Wheels & Final Details
Press-fit all four wheels onto the axle stubs. Apply decals precisely using tweezers — work slowly and use a damp cloth to reposition if needed.
